# Ingredients
1. 1/2 tsp Cream of tartar
2. 3tbsp + 1tsp Veg Oil
3. 100g Cake flour
4. (45g batter, 55g meringue) Sugar
5. 85ml Coconut milk
6. 1tbsp pandan juice -> 10 pandan leaves, Pandan juice / 5tbsp water
7. 1/4-1/2 tsp pandan paste
8. 1/4 tsp salt
9. 1 tsp Baking powder
10. 5 eggs


# Procedure
1. Extract pandan juice: 
a) Cut into 2cm sections, place in blender and add water, blend until fine pulp.
b) Scoopt out pulp into metal sieve and strain it.
c) Set aside amount needed and store rest in air tight jar and chill up to 5 days

2. Make batter:
a) In a large mixing bowl, Whisk egg yolks and sugar until thick and fluffy
b) stir in coconut milk, oil, pandan juice and pandan paste. Stir with a whisk until well combined
c) Add sifted flour, baking powder and salt mixture in 2 additions
d) stir with a whisk until all flour is incorporated and no streaks of flour are visible. Mixture should be thick, smooth and free of lumps.

3. Whip up meringue
a) In electric mixer with whisk attachment, pour egg whites and sprinkle cream of tartar over
b) Whisk on medium speed until the whites start to turn frothy.
c) add sugar bit by bit in a steady stream.
d) whip until stiff peaks form. This may take 5 - 7 mins, depending on mixer and speed.

4. Fold meringue into batter
a) Add 1/3 of meringue to batter. Fold in gently with a whisk until well incorporated.
b) add another 1/3 and do same as above.
c) add the rest and fold gently until there is no visible streaks of meringue

5. Baking and cooling
a) Baking - 170 degrees for 45 mins
b) open only 5 mins before end of baking and insert skewer to see if it comes out clean
c) Invert after it is done and let it cool


# Equipment Required
1. Filtering Sieve
2. tube pan (8 inch)
3. Electric mixer
4. Spoon (tbsp, tsp)
5. Cup, mixing bowl
